A portable Claude skill that turns the model into a senior designer — grounded in real context, committed to a visual system, refusing to make AI-slop.

The skill's entire premise: a committed system beats restrained guesswork every time. Hedging reads as confusion. Half-committing to a style produces something worse than the most basic Swiss grid. When you go, go.

A
SKILL,
plus
some
rules.

Claude Design is a SKILL.md plus a handful of on-demand references that Claude loads when your request sounds like design. It does not add features. It removes unhelpful defaults.

Instead of reaching for a gradient, the skill asks Claude to gather what is real. Instead of guessing at a brand, it hunts down actual assets. Instead of improvising when the brief is vague, it offers three directions and waits.

Three rules,
no hedging.

01.

Verify first. Always.

When a product is named, search before assuming. 10-second search beats 1–2 hours of rework. Hard rule. Not a preference.

02.

Grab the real stuff.

Ask → search → download → verify → freeze. Logo, product shots, UI screenshots FIRST. Colors and fonts second. Not the other way around.

03.

Three, then commit.

Brief too vague? Three differentiated directions, rendered as preview sketches. User picks. Then — and this is the important part — commit fully.

One line.
Go wild.
