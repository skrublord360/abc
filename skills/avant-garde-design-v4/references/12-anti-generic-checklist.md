# Anti-Generic Checklist & "AI Slop" Filter

## 1.0 The "Safe Harbor" Forbidden List
In 2026, these patterns signal generic, template-driven, or AI-generated design. Avoid them unless you can reinvent them completely.

| Pattern | Why It's Generic | Avant-Garde Alternative |
|---------|------------------|-------------------------|
| **Bento Grids** | Modern cliché, every AI design. | Question the grid; try asymmetry or vertical narrative. |
| **Hero Split (L/R)** | Predictable, boring. | Massive typography or vertical stack. |
| **Mesh Gradients** | Lazy "floating blobs". | Radical color pairing or high-contrast flat. |
| **Glassmorphism** | AI's idea of "premium". | Intentional color stories, solid tactile surfaces. |
| **Inter/Roboto Default** | Safe = forgettable. | Distinctive type pairings with purpose. |
| **Purple/Indigo Blur** | Overused cliché. | Unexpected combinations (e.g., Cream/Stone/Gold). |

---

## 2.0 Complete Anti-Generic Checklist (10 Points)

Before marking a UI work complete, it must pass these 10 points:

- [ ] **1. Intentionality:** Every element earns its place (Why? Only? Without?).
- [ ] **2. Distinctive Hierarchy:** Large typography is used for more than just size (e.g., as a structural element).
- [ ] **3. Whitespace as Voice:** Whitespace communicates drama or calm, not just empty space.
- [ ] **4. Human Imperfection:** Intentional roughness or asymmetry that signals authorship.
- [ ] **5. Tactile Interaction:** Elements feel physically reactive (shimmers, squish, weight).
- [ ] **6. Radical Color:** Palette deviates from the standard "SaaS Blue/Indigo".
- [ ] **7. Narrative Flow:** The page tells a story, rather than just listing features.
- [ ] **8. Typography Soul:** Fonts are selected for character, not just legibility.
- [ ] **9. Invisible UX:** Micro-interactions serve the user, not just the eyes.
- [ ] **10. Strategic Alignment:** The aesthetic directly supports the "Compass" position.

## 3.0 Scoring System (The Quality Gate)

Evaluate your design from 1-10 on these three metrics:

| Metric | Score (1-10) | Meaning |
|--------|--------------|---------|
| **Memorability** | | Will the user remember this site in 24 hours? |
| **Integrity** | | Does the code and UI feel like a cohesive whole? |
| **Craftsmanship** | | Are the details (spacing, timing, contrast) flawless? |

**Pass Threshold:** Minimum **24/30** total score.

---

## 4.0 The "Universal Truths" Filter

If your design fails any of these, it's not yet Avant-Garde:
1. **HIERARCHY:** Test by squinting—if hierarchy collapses, redesign.
2. **INTENTIONALITY:** If you cannot articulate *why* an element exists, delete it.
3. **WHITESPACE:** Before adding an element, ask: "Could whitespace achieve this goal instead?"

---

## 5.0 Design Prompt Library (2026 Edition)

Use these prompts to push your design further:
- *"If our brand were a person, would they be a tenured professor or a startup founder?"*
- *"If our headlines were spoken, would they be whispered or shouted?"*
- *"If we removed all color, would our design still communicate brand?"*
- *"Does our grid feel like a comfortable home or an exciting city?"*
- *"Where do we want the user's eye to struggle?" (intentional tension)*
